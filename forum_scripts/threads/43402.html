First of all don't keep a reference to your object in your Controller. Your Controller is a singletond and not threadsafe! So when 2 request come in your AddPatient object from request 1 is overwritten with the one from request 2. The same goes for your isPostback property.

Also when you return to the Controller the instance in your Controller is a different instance then the one returned from the session. (Due to Serialization and Deserialization).

Also as Steve O pointed out, the country list is reference data and shouldn't be part of your commandObject. Your command object should only contain those fields which you want to enter in your form (i.e. name, number, date of birth etc.).

I'm not sure what you are doing in your onFormChange method but I think you need to take a look at how to use PropertyEditors.

All the comments mentioned above give me the faint idea that you don't yet grasp the needed basic of the SpringMVC framework. Therefor I strongly suggest that you take a look at the sample application shipped with Spring and that you read the reference guide. Especially chapter 13 which covers SpringMVC

Edit: You seem to be using/extending the PetClinic sample from spring. I strongly suggest that you take a look at all the controllers and other code regarding that sample. That should clarify a lot of issues.

You probably have an error in your jsp. You probably made the names/ids of the input fields up yourself. As I stated before closely study the samples.

Code:
<spring:bind name="command.username">
  <input type="text" name="${status.expression}" value="${status.value}"
</spring:bind>
However I suggest you read-up on the new spring 2.0 form tags.

Code:
<form:input path="username" />
Which is the same as the earlier mentioned spring bind tags.
