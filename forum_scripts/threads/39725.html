Well, assuming that you understand how to set up a controller, and how it corresponds to a JSP (view), here's approximately how you'd do it:
Quote:
This .jsp-page shall display the mentioned context factors (they shall be imported out of the not working method).
This would be handled in your controller's referenceData method.
Quote:
Afterwards I want to select via checkboxes several context factors and those, which are selected, I want to send via a form to another jsp-page.
What do you mean by you "want to send the selected checkboxes"? This could be as simple as setting the form ACTION in your JSP to point to another JSP, which is mapped to a separate controller. i.e. the first JSP (J1) depends on controller C1 for backing data. The form in J1 has a form action corresponding to a second JSP (J2), which depends on controller C2 for backing data.

I'd suggest reading through the excellent docs in Spring 2 on how to use Web MVC and revisit the structure of your controllers again. It seems like you aren't really understanding how it all fits together - for example it is not possible for JSPs to refer directly to methods in your controller (it's really a violation of MVC principles anyway). Try going through some of the documentation and examples, and then take a look at your code again.

What you're trying to do sounds pretty straightforward, though.
